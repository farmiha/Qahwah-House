Our Story

Eight generations of coffee mastery, from Yemen to the world

The Beginning

Where Coffee
Was Born

Long before coffee became the world's most beloved beverage, it was a Yemeni secret — cultivated on terraced mountain farms, brewed in family homes, and shared as a symbol of hospitality and community.

Qahwah House traces its roots to the ancient coffee-growing highlands of Yemen, where our family has cultivated premium Arabica coffee beans for over eight generations. As pioneers of the world's first coffee tradition, we bring centuries of expertise to every single cup we serve.

The word "Qahwah" (قهوة) is the original Arabic word that gave rise to "coffee" in every language on earth. Yemen didn't just grow coffee — Yemen invented coffee culture. We carry that legacy with immense pride.

"Coffee was born in Yemen. Qahwah House brings that birthright to every cup."
Our Journey

Eight Generations,
One Mission

1800s

The First Harvest

Our ancestors planted the first Arabica coffee trees on the slopes of the Al Hasbani mountain range in Yemen. These ancient trees still bear fruit today.

1950s

Expanding the Farm

The fifth generation expanded operations, introducing sustainable farming practices and organic cultivation methods maintained to this day.

2000s

Coming to America

The seventh generation brought their family's legacy to the United States, opening the first Qahwah House in Dearborn, Michigan.

2010s

The Revolution Begins

Word spread fast. Qahwah House became the epicenter of the Yemeni coffee revolution in America, inspiring a new generation of appreciation for authentic specialty coffee.

Today

30+ Locations & Growing

With over 30 locations across the United States, Qahwah House continues its mission: to share authentic Yemeni coffee culture with the world, one sip at a time.

What We Stand For

Our Mission

Authenticity

We never compromise on the authentic Yemeni coffee experience. Every recipe, every blend, every tradition is preserved exactly as our ancestors intended.
